Clock Tower

Built in the first half of the XVI century in the centre of the city, the Clock tower was used as a watch tower by the Ottoman authorities. By the end of XVII century, in the period of the reformation in Veles, the tower was transformed into a city clock. The bell of the tower could have been heard within a radius of 25km.

Kocho Racin Memorial House

The house of one of the greatest Macedonian poets between the two World Wars, Kosta Solev Racin, was built on the left side of the Vardar River and represents a traditional Veles house, which today is considered the type of house with the most attractive architecture. The memorial house was completely reconstructed in 1962.

Ossuary Monument

The Ossuary Monument in Veles was built to commemorate the Partisan soldiers who fought for the freedom of Veles and Macedonia during the National Liberation War from 1941 to 1945. In addition, this monument serves as a resting place for the remains of roughly one hundred fallen Partisan soldiers from the Veles area.

Theater "Dzinot"

Founded in 1948, the National Institution Center of Culture "Jordan HK-Dzinot" is one of the oldest and leading theater institutions in North Macedonia. The theater is named after Jordan Hadzi Konstantinov - Dzinot - the first Macedonian playwright and founder of the Macedonian theater. The theater has a long and rich history of 70 years of production.

Lake Mladost

Situated 8km north from the town Veles, in the surroundings of the Otovica village, the artificial lake Mladost represents one of the most visited destinations in the area. It is strongly recommended for transit travelers, as a place for a short brake.

Church of St. Panteleimon

The Church of St. Panteleimon is considered to be one of the pearls of the sacral architecture in the Balkans. The church was built from 1840 to 1847 by the famous Macedonian church builder Andrej Damjanov together with his brothers. Since 2009, St. Panteleimon has been proclaimed a saint-protector of the city of Veles.

Gemidzii Monument

The imposing Gemidzii monument represents an involution of 12 sun rays which symbolize the twelve members of the revolutionary group Gemidzii, who in the beginning of the Ilinden Uprising made famous attacks and bombings of Salonica, while trying to draw the attention of Europe to the Macedonian question.
